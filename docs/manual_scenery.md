# Editable world and manual scenery workflow

The course, terrain, ocean, bridge, flyovers and tunnel structure remain generated by code. All existing decorative scenery is saved in an editable district scene, and additional catalog scenery can be placed manually.

## Edit the existing world

1. Open `scenes/world/editable_world.tscn` and switch to the 3D workspace.
2. Wait for the locked road, terrain, water, bridge, flyover and tunnel preview to appear.
3. Expand a district such as `CityCentre`, `PartyTown`, `NorthCoast`, `Waterfront` or `PartyIsland` in the Scene tree.
4. Select a building, palm, lamp, boat, poster or other object root. Press `F` to focus it.
5. Use `W` to move, `E` to rotate and `Ctrl+D` to duplicate. Moving a root moves its complete compound model together.
6. Press `Ctrl+S`. The game loads this saved scene directly, so edits appear at runtime without procedural duplicates.

The saved objects are grouped into districts. Buildings are arranged in named, road-relative blocks: front and rear rows use consistent spacing and setbacks, while the rear row changes the building order for variety. Each building remains an independent compound object, so you can move one without selecting the entire district. Unique landmarks such as the lighthouse remain single objects.

`NeighborhoodDetails` is an additive generated scene containing the connective layer around every block and standalone building: paired roadside lamps, sidewalks, rear walks, driveways, fences, bushes, docks and small boats. `NaturalLandscapes` contains the first six hills, dunes, mountain and oasis compounds. Both appear inside the locked `GeneratedWorldPreview` while editing and are loaded at identity at runtime. They are intentionally not stored as selectable children of `editable_world.tscn`, preventing an accidental click from moving or burying an entire generated layer. Use catalog presets for hand-authored paths, plants or fences you want to adjust individually.

Regenerate the additive layers after moving many buildings:

```powershell
.\godot\Godot_v4.7-stable_win64_console.exe --path . --headless --script res://tools/generate_neighborhood_details.gd
.\godot\Godot_v4.7-stable_win64_console.exe --path . --headless --script res://tools/generate_natural_landscapes.gd
```

Do not run `tools/generate_editable_world.gd` after beginning manual edits: that developer tool recreates the baseline scene and would replace those edits.

## Drag and drop

1. Open `scenes/world/editable_world.tscn` in Godot.
2. Wait for `Editor world preview` in the Output panel. The complete generated road, terrain, water, bridges, tunnels, districts and existing decorations are now visible but locked.
3. Expand `scenes/manual_scenery/presets` in the FileSystem panel. Presets are grouped by buildings, landmarks, vegetation, street props, signs, boats and sky scenery.
4. Drag a `.tscn` preset into the 3D viewport or onto `EditableWorld/ManualScenery` in the Scene tree.
5. Ensure the new instance is a **direct child** of `ManualScenery`.
6. Use Godot's move and rotate tools. Every building faces local `-Z`; rotate its Y axis toward the road.

The infrastructure preview is editor-only, has no physics and is never saved into the scene or included twice in the game. Select `EditorPlacementGuide` to disable `Show World Preview` on slower computers.

The lightweight guides remain available through `Show Land Corridor` and `Show Water Plane`. They only exist in the editor and add no runtime meshes.

## Inspector controls and warnings

- Every preset exposes its footprint, surface and catalog identity.
- Billboard, wall-poster and aircraft presets expose `Poster Texture`; assigning another image updates their visible faces.
- Aircraft expose optional straight-line motion. They remain stationary unless `Movement Enabled` is checked.
- Yellow warning icons identify road intrusion, overlapping manual scenery, incorrect water/air height, invalid scale or a preset outside `ManualScenery`.
- Manual scenery is visual-only by design. It contains no collision bodies, so decoration cannot unexpectedly stop or catapult the car.

For water props, start near `Y = -1.4`. Land foundations use local `Y = 0`. Connectable paths, fences and surface pieces intentionally allow overlap. Traffic cones, barricades and crosswalks may be placed on the road and remain visual-only.

The catalog now contains 164 draggable scenes: the original buildings and landmarks, 52 individual landscaping/street/beach pieces, and 55 friend-media variants. Under `presets/friend_media`, every one of the 11 current images has a billboard, wall poster, zeppelin, banner plane and standalone air banner. `Poster Texture` remains editable on every copy.

## Refreshing the checked-in catalog

When the factory or catalog changes, regenerate the PackedScenes with:

```powershell
.\godot\Godot_v4.7-stable_win64_console.exe --path . --headless --script res://tools/generate_manual_scenery_catalog.gd
```

The generated `.tscn` files are committed so they have native Godot thumbnails and the exact same geometry in the editor and the game.
