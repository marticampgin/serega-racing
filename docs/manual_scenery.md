# Manual scenery workflow

The course, terrain, ocean, bridge, flyovers and tunnel structure remain generated by code. Decorative scenery can be placed manually without changing that system.

## Drag and drop

1. Open `scenes/main.tscn` in Godot.
2. Expand `scenes/manual_scenery/presets` in the FileSystem panel. Presets are grouped by buildings, landmarks, vegetation, street props, signs, boats and sky scenery.
3. Drag a `.tscn` preset into the 3D viewport or onto `Race/ManualScenery` in the Scene tree.
4. Ensure the new instance is a **direct child** of `ManualScenery`.
5. Use Godot's move and rotate tools. Every building faces local `-Z`; rotate its Y axis toward the road.

The blue plane marks sea level, the grey ribbon is the generated road, the pink line is its centre and the translucent coral ribbon is an approximate land-placement corridor. These guides only exist in the editor and add no runtime meshes.

## Inspector controls and warnings

- Every preset exposes its footprint, surface and catalog identity.
- Billboard, wall-poster and aircraft presets expose `Poster Texture`; assigning another image updates their visible faces.
- Aircraft expose optional straight-line motion. They remain stationary unless `Movement Enabled` is checked.
- Yellow warning icons identify road intrusion, overlapping manual scenery, incorrect water/air height, invalid scale or a preset outside `ManualScenery`.
- Manual scenery is visual-only by design. It contains no collision bodies, so decoration cannot unexpectedly stop or catapult the car.

For water props, start near `Y = -1.4`. Land foundations use local `Y = 0`. Buildings, signs and plants reserve their footprint before procedural scenery is populated, so generated buildings avoid manually decorated plots.

## Refreshing the checked-in catalog

When the factory or catalog changes, regenerate the PackedScenes with:

```powershell
.\godot\Godot_v4.7-stable_win64_console.exe --path . --headless --script res://tools/generate_manual_scenery_catalog.gd
```

The generated `.tscn` files are committed so they have native Godot thumbnails and the exact same geometry in the editor and the game.
